I am a climate scientist (to know more about me, head here) and have been using Python as my main high-level language for data analysis and visualisation for about 10 years, slowly transitioning from Matlab and R.



In this blog, I will try and share what's I have learned along the way, and give full examples of how I use Python in my research or operational workflows.



I will occasionally reflect on open science, and how open-source in general, and Python in particular can be used to help make climate science more open.
